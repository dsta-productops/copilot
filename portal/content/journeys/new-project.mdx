You're in your first week leading a new project. There's a brief from management, maybe a few decks, possibly some informal conversations. What you don't have yet: a defined product, validated users, or a documented decision about *what* to build.

The temptation will be to start solutioning. Resist it for two or three days. The work below is the cheapest part of the whole project — and the part with the highest leverage on everything that follows.
