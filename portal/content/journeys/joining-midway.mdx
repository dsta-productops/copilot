Joining mid-flight is harder than starting fresh, but the reflex to "redo the research properly" is almost always wrong. Prior work is the team's accumulated context — discarding it costs the team weeks and signals you don't trust their judgment.

The work below is about reading the artefacts the team has already produced, understanding which loop iteration they're in, and finding a contribution slice that helps rather than disrupts. Resist forming strong opinions until you've done all six steps.
