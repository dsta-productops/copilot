Use this prompt to generate a complete test plan — including scenarios — in one pass. The output is a single document a team can read, agree to, and run from.

## Tips

- The "Objective" sentence is the test of the test plan. If you can't write the objective in one sentence, the test isn't focused enough.
- Scenarios should exercise success criteria, not just demo the artefact. A scenario where the participant cannot fail isn't a test.
- "Validity risks" is the section teams under-invest in. Common ones: participants too close to the team, the prototype lacking content for the task, observers' presence changing behaviour, success criteria that depend on environmental factors the test can't reproduce. Name them up front.
- For digital tests, the DASH 2.0 prototype survey runs immediately after the session. For engineering or deployed-system tests, the DASH system survey runs after sufficient exposure. The test plan should name which one and why.
- Findings flow directly into the next design iteration — there is no separate synthesis prompt for test data. Captures live in DASH 2.0; design adjustments live in the next [prototype-from-prd](/prompts/prototype-from-prd) run or its engineering equivalent.
